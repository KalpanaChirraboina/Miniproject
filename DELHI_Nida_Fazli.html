Nida Fazli

Renowned Urdu Poet and Lyricist

Born: October 12, 1938, Delhi

Died: February 8, 2016, Mumbai

State: Delhi

Language: Urdu, Hindi

Nationality: Indian

Education: Graduate, University of Delhi

Occupation: Poet, Lyricist, Writer

Notable Role: Prominent Modern Urdu Poet and Bollywood Lyricist

Nida Fazli was a celebrated Urdu poet and Bollywood lyricist whose evocative ghazals and songs blended simplicity with profound emotion. Born in Delhi, his work reflected themes of love, loss, and the human condition, resonating with diverse audiences. His contributions to Urdu poetry and Indian cinema, including iconic film songs, made him a beloved figure in modern Indian literature.

Books and Publications

  • Dhoop Kiran (1973): A collection of ghazals and poems.
  • Safar Mein Dhoop (1983): Reflective poetry on life’s journey.
  • Khamoshi Ki Aawaz (1998): A compilation of introspective works.
  • Mulaqatein (2000): A collection of poems and dohas.

Early Life and Education

Nida Fazli was born on October 12, 1938, in Delhi to a family with a rich literary tradition. After the Partition of India, his family moved to Pakistan, but he chose to stay in India. He graduated from the University of Delhi and was influenced by Urdu literary circles, developing a unique style that blended traditional and modern poetic forms.

Literary Career

Nida Fazli’s career spanned poetry, prose, and Bollywood lyrics. His ghazals, known for their simplicity and emotional depth, gained widespread acclaim. He also wrote dohas, reviving the traditional Hindi poetic form. His Bollywood songs for films like Razia Sultan and Sarfarosh showcased his lyrical versatility, making him a household name.

Social and Political Activism

Nida Fazli’s poetry often reflected social and cultural concerns, addressing themes of communal harmony and human struggles. While not an overt activist, his works subtly critiqued societal divides and celebrated India’s pluralistic ethos, making him a voice for unity and introspection in a diverse nation.

Selected Poems

  • Kabhi Kisi Ko Muqammal Jahan Nahi Milta (1973): A reflective ghazal on life’s imperfections.
  • Har Taraf Har Jagah (1983): A poem on human connection.
  • Duniya Jise Kehte Hain (1998): A critique of materialism.
  • Hosh Walon Ko Khabar Kya (1999): A popular Bollywood song from Sarfarosh.

Major Achievements

  • Received the Sahitya Akademi Award for Khamoshi Ki Aawaz (1998).
  • Honored with the Padma Shri (2013) for contributions to literature.
  • Wrote iconic Bollywood songs for films like Razia Sultan and Sarfarosh.
  • Revived the doha form in modern Urdu poetry.

Legacy and Honors

Nida Fazli’s poetry and lyrics continue to resonate with audiences for their emotional depth and accessibility. His works are celebrated in mushairas and literary festivals, and his contributions to Bollywood have left a lasting impact. Awards like the Nida Fazli Award honor his legacy in Urdu literature.

Famous Quotes

“No one ever finds a perfect world.”
“What do the sane know of the madness of love?”
“Every place, every corner, is filled with the stories of hearts.”

Personal Life and Children

Nida Fazli was married and had a daughter. He lived a private life, focusing on his literary and lyrical work. After moving to Mumbai, he became deeply involved in the film industry while maintaining his commitment to Urdu poetry, balancing personal and professional spheres with humility.
