Gaddar

Gummadi Vittal Rao

The People's Bard

Born: January 31, 1949, Tupran, Telangana

Died: August 6, 2023, Hyderabad

State: Telangana

Language: Telugu

Nationality: Indian

Education: Engineering (incomplete), Osmania University

Occupation: Poet, Balladeer, Activist

Notable Role: Founder, Jana Natya Mandali

Gummadi Vittal Rao, popularly known as Gaddar, was a revolutionary Telugu poet, balladeer, and activist from Telangana, celebrated as the voice of the marginalized. His powerful songs and performances, rooted in folk traditions, mobilized masses for social justice, the Naxalite movement, and Telangana statehood. Founder of Jana Natya Mandali, Gaddar's work blended Marxist and Ambedkarite ideologies, addressing caste oppression and labor exploitation, leaving an indelible mark on Telugu culture and activism.

Books and Publications

  • My Life is a Song: Gaddar’s Anthems for Revolution (2021): Translated by Vasanth Kannabiran, a collection of his songs and stories.
  • Prati Paataku Oka Katha Unda? Aa Undi (1991): A book of song stories (limited availability).
  • Taragani Gani: Prajala Patala Puttupuvottaraalu (1992): History of people’s songs (limited availability).

Early Life and Education

Born on January 31, 1949, in Tupran, Medak district, Telangana, to Dalit laborers Lacchumamma and Seshaiah, Gaddar grew up in poverty. Influenced by his father’s Ambedkarite ideals, he became the first Dalit boy in his village to pass higher secondary school. He enrolled in engineering at Osmania University but dropped out due to financial constraints, later briefly working at Canara Bank before embracing revolutionary politics.

Political Career

Gaddar was a key figure in the Naxalite movement, joining the CPI (Marxist-Leninist) People’s War in the 1980s and living underground for a decade. He founded the Telangana Praja Front in 2010 to advocate for marginalized communities during the Telangana movement. Later, he embraced electoral politics, voting in 2018 and campaigning for Congress, aiming to protect constitutional values. He announced plans for a Praja Party before his death in 2023.

Literary Contributions

Gaddar composed nearly 3,000 songs, blending Telugu folk with revolutionary themes of caste, class, and social justice. His works, often credited to Jana Natya Mandali, include hits like “Podustunna Poddu Meeda” and “Bandenaka Bandi Katti.” His poetry and songs mobilized Dalits and laborers, notably during the 1985 Karamchedu massacre, and inspired the Telangana movement. He also wrote in Hindi and Gond languages.

Selected Songs

  • Podustunna Poddu Meeda (2009): Anthem of the Telangana movement, featured in “Jai Bolo Telangana.”
  • Bandenaka Bandi Katti (1979): Revolutionary song from the film “Maa Bhoomi.”
  • Amma Telanganama (1996): Iconic song galvanizing the Telangana agitation.
  • Karamchedu Song (1985): Mobilized Dalits post-Karamchedu massacre.
  • Mandal Song (1990): Pro-Mandal Commission song supporting reservations.

Major Achievements

  • Founded Jana Natya Mandali, cultural wing of CPI (ML) People’s War (1972).
  • Received ₹1 crore posthumous award for Telangana movement contributions (2023).
  • Mobilized masses during the 1985 Karamchedu massacre and 1990 Mandal protests.
    • Led Telangana Praja Front to unite marginalized groups (2010).
  • Inspired Telangana statehood through songs and performances (1969–2014).

Legacy and Honors

Gaddar’s songs and activism continue to inspire social justice movements. His legacy is honored through the Gaddar Awards, replacing Nandi Awards, and a statue planned in Tellapur, Hyderabad. His contributions to Telangana’s statehood and Dalit rights remain iconic, with his funeral in 2023 conducted with state honors, reflecting his profound impact.

Famous Quotes

“My name is Gummadi Vittal. Gaddar is the name of my song.”
“Profit is my God; Cheap Labour is my Country, and Market is my Philosophy.”
“If 25 percent of the Constitution had been implemented, armed struggle would not be needed.”

Timeline of Important Events

  • 1949: Born in Tupran, Telangana.
  • 1972: Founded Jana Natya Mandali.
  • 1975: Arrested during Emergency, later went underground.
  • 1985: Mobilized Dalits post-Karamchedu massacre.
  • 1997: Survived assassination attempt, bullet lodged in spine.
  • 2010: Founded Telangana Praja Front, disassociated from Maoists.
  • 2018: Voted for the first time in Telangana elections.
  • 2023: Passed away in Hyderabad.

Personal Life and Children

Gaddar married Vimala and had three children: sons Surya and Chandra, and daughter Vennela. Despite his revolutionary life, he maintained family ties. His daughter Vennela contested the 2023 Telangana elections with Congress. Gaddar lived with a bullet in his spine from a 1997 assassination attempt, symbolizing his resilience against state repression.
