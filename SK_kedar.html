Early Life and Education

Kedar Nath Gurung was born on July 9, 1948, in the village of Sardong, Lingchom, in Sikkim. He completed his early schooling at a local school in Lingchom before enrolling at Turnbull High School in Darjeeling, where he passed his matriculation examination in 1960. He later graduated from Government College, Darjeeling, laying the foundation for his career in education and literature.

Literary Career

Gurung is renowned for his satirical and poignant Nepalese literature, with 15 publications spanning poetry, essays, and literary criticism. His poetry collections, such as Aja Pheri Auta Samajhna Ra Naya Dinharoo (1970) and Mahashathiharooko Pooja (1979), explore social, cultural, and spiritual themes. He served as the Chief Editor of Shrashta magazine for 22 years and was a former editor of Jhilka and Prakashan. Gurung also contributed to the Nepali Sahitya Akademi as a member from 1983 to 1987.
