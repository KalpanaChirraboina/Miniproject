Yishey Doma is a celebrated journalist, author, and poet from Sikkim, known for her evocative storytelling that captures the essence of Himalayan culture. As the founder of the Sikkim Chronicle, she has documented the region’s history, folklore, and contemporary issues. Her literary works, blending poetry and prose, explore themes of identity, nature, and social change, earning her recognition as a powerful voice in Sikkimese literature.

Early Life and Education

Yishey Doma was born in Gangtok, Sikkim, to a family deeply rooted in the state’s cultural heritage. She completed her early schooling in Sikkim before pursuing higher education at Delhi University, where she studied literature. Her exposure to diverse literary traditions shaped her writing style, blending Sikkimese sensibilities with broader Indian narratives.

Literary and Journalistic Career

Yishey began her career as a journalist, contributing to regional and national publications. She founded the Sikkim Chronicle, a platform dedicated to documenting Sikkim’s socio-cultural and political landscape. Her books, including Sikkim: The Story of the Fortune Warriors (2008) and Legends of Sikkim (2010), weave historical and folk narratives. Her poetry, though less documented, reflects themes of nature, identity, and resilience.

Literary Contributions

Yishey Doma’s works are a bridge between Sikkim’s oral traditions and modern literature. Her historical narratives, like Sikkim: The Story of the Fortune Warriors, preserve the state’s legacy, while Legends of Sikkim revives folklore. Her poetry, often unpublished, explores the Himalayan ethos, earning her acclaim as a cultural storyteller.
