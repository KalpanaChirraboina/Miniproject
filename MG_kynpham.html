Kynpham Sing Nongkynrih Profile Details

Kynpham Sing Nongkynrih, born in 1964 in Sohra, Meghalaya, is a leading Khasi poet, author, and translator, celebrated for his bilingual works in Khasi and English. A professor at North-Eastern Hill University, his poetry blends Khasi oral traditions with contemporary themes, exploring identity, nature, and social issues. Honored with the Sahitya Akademi Award in 2018, Nongkynrih’s contributions have elevated Khasi literature on national and global stages.

Books and Publications

  • The Yearning of Seeds (2011): A celebrated collection of English poems.
  • Time’s Barter: Haiku and Senryu (2015): A collection of haiku and senryu poems.
  • Funeral Nights (2021): A novel exploring Khasi culture and traditions.

Early Life and Education

Born on April 4, 1964, in Sohra, Meghalaya, Kynpham Sing Nongkynrih grew up immersed in Khasi oral traditions. He pursued his M.A. and Ph.D. at North-Eastern Hill University, Shillong, specializing in English literature. His academic background and exposure to both Khasi folklore and global literary traditions shaped his unique bilingual poetic style.

Literary Career

Nongkynrih began his literary career with Moments (1992), establishing himself as a leading English poet from Northeast India. His works, including The Yearning of Seeds (2011) and Funeral Nights (2021), blend Khasi sensibilities with universal themes. As a translator, he has brought Khasi literature to wider audiences, notably through Around the Hearth (2000).

Literary Contributions

Nongkynrih’s bilingual poetry in Khasi and English explores themes of nature, identity, and social change, rooted in Khasi matrilineal traditions. His translations, such as Hiraeth (2021), have introduced Khasi folklore to global readers. His novel Funeral Nights offers a vivid portrayal of Khasi culture, earning critical acclaim for its narrative depth.

Legacy and Honors

Kynpham Sing Nongkynrih’s works have elevated Khasi literature globally, earning him recognition as a leading voice from Northeast India. His Sahitya Akademi Award and inclusion in anthologies like The Oxford Anthology of Writings from North-East India underscore his impact. His writings continue to inspire young Khasi poets and writers.
