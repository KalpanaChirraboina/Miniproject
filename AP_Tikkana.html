Tikkana Profile Details

Tikkana, also known as Tikkana Somayaji, was a 13th-century Telugu poet and scholar, celebrated as one of the Kavi Trayam (trinity of poets) who translated the Mahabharata into Telugu. Serving as a minister in the court of King Manuma Siddhi II, Tikkana’s monumental work, Mahabharatam, enriched Telugu literature with its poetic depth and philosophical insights, earning him the title "Kavi Brahma" (Divine Poet).

Books and Publications

  • Telugu Mahabharatam (13th Century): Translation of 15 parvas of the Mahabharata.
  • Nirvachanottara Ramayanamu (13th Century): A poetic retelling of the Ramayana.

Early Life and Education

Tikkana was born around 1205 in Nellore, Andhra Pradesh, to a Telugu Brahmin family. A scholar of Sanskrit and Telugu, he was trained in Vedic literature and classical texts. His erudition and poetic talent earned him a prominent position as a minister in the court of King Manuma Siddhi II of the Nellore Chola dynasty.

Literary Career

Tikkana’s most significant contribution is his translation of 15 parvas (books) of the Mahabharata into Telugu, part of the Kavi Trayam’s collaborative effort with Nannaya and Yerrapragada. His Mahabharatam is renowned for its lyrical beauty, moral depth, and accessibility, making the epic a cornerstone of Telugu literature. He also authored Nirvachanottara Ramayanamu, a scholarly retelling of the Ramayana.

Social and Political Activism

As a minister, Tikkana played a key role in the administration of the Nellore Chola kingdom. His poetry reflected social and ethical values, promoting dharma and justice. By translating the Mahabharata, he made complex philosophical ideas accessible to the common people, fostering cultural and moral awareness.

Selected Poems

  • Mahabharatam – Virata Parvam: A vivid poetic translation of the Virata Parva.
  • Mahabharatam – Udyoga Parvam: Verses on diplomatic efforts before the Kurukshetra war.
  • Mahabharatam – Bhishma Parvam: Poetic depiction of Bhishma’s role in the war.
  • Nirvachanottara Ramayanamu: A concise poetic retelling of the Ramayana.
  • Mahabharatam – Drona Parvam: Poetic narration of Drona’s exploits in the war.

Major Achievements

  • Translated 15 parvas of the Mahabharata, a monumental contribution to Telugu literature.
  • Earned the title "Kavi Brahma" for his poetic excellence.
  • Authored Nirvachanottara Ramayanamu, enriching Telugu devotional literature.
  • Strengthened Telugu literary traditions as part of the Kavi Trayam.
  • Influenced generations of poets and scholars with his lyrical style.

Legacy and Honors

Tikkana’s Mahabharatam remains a foundational text in Telugu literature, studied and revered for its poetic and philosophical richness. His contributions are celebrated through literary festivals and academic studies in Andhra Pradesh. The Kavi Trayam’s legacy, led by Tikkana, is a cornerstone of Telugu cultural identity.

Famous Quotes

Personal Life and Children

Tikkana, a devout Brahmin, led a life dedicated to scholarship and service. Details about his wife and children are scarce, but he was known for his piety and commitment to Vedic traditions. His role as a minister and poet reflected his balanced approach to worldly and spiritual duties.
