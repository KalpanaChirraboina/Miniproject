Sukumar Ray

The Master of Nonsense Verse

Born: October 30, 1887, Kolkata, West Bengal

Died: September 10, 1923, Kolkata, West Bengal

State: West Bengal

Language: Bengali

Nationality: Indian

Education: Presidency College, Kolkata; studied in England

Occupation: Poet, Writer, Illustrator, Publisher

Notable Role: Editor of Sandesh magazine

Sukumar Ray was a pioneering Bengali poet and writer from West Bengal, celebrated for his whimsical nonsense verse and children’s literature. Known for his playful yet profound poems and stories, he created a unique genre blending humor, satire, and imagination. His iconic work, Abol Tabol, and his role in editing Sandesh magazine have made him a beloved figure in Bengali culture, influencing generations of readers and writers.

Books and Publications

  • Abol Tabol (1923): A collection of nonsense poems, his most famous work.
  • HaJaBaRaLa (1921): A whimsical children’s story.
  • Pagla Dashu (1920s): A collection of humorous short stories (available on Archive.org).
  • Lakkhaner Shaktishel (1922): A satirical play.

Early Life and Education

Sukumar Ray was born on October 30, 1887, in Kolkata, West Bengal, to a prominent literary family. His father, Upendrakishore Ray Chowdhury, was a writer and publisher. Sukumar studied physics and chemistry at Presidency College, Kolkata, and later trained in printing technology in England. His exposure to Western literature, especially Edward Lear and Lewis Carroll, shaped his unique nonsense style.

Political Career

Sukumar Ray did not engage in formal politics but subtly critiqued societal norms and colonial rule through his satirical and humorous writings. His works, like Lakkhaner Shaktishel, used wit to comment on bureaucracy and social issues, aligning with the cultural nationalism of early 20th-century Bengal, particularly during the Swadeshi movement.

Literary Contributions

Sukumar Ray revolutionized Bengali children’s literature with his nonsense verse and stories, blending humor, wordplay, and social satire. His masterpiece, Abol Tabol, is a landmark in Bengali poetry, while HaJaBaRaLa and Pagla Dashu captivated young readers. As editor of Sandesh, he promoted children’s literature, leaving a lasting legacy in Bengali culture.

Selected Poems

  • Kumropotash (1923): A whimsical poem from Abol Tabol.
  • Bhondar Hawa (1923): A playful nonsense verse.
  • Tuntunir Boi (1923): A humorous poem about a tailor bird.
  • Gondho Bichar (1923): A satirical poem from Abol Tabol.
  • Heshoram Hushiarer Diary (1920s): A quirky narrative poem.

Major Achievements

  • Authored Abol Tabol, a cornerstone of Bengali nonsense literature.
  • Revived and edited Sandesh, a leading children’s magazine.
  • Pioneered nonsense verse in Bengali, influencing children’s literature.
  • Created iconic characters like Pagla Dashu and Heshoram Hushiar.
  • Introduced advanced printing techniques to Bengali publishing.

Legacy and Honors

Sukumar Ray’s legacy endures through his timeless nonsense verse and stories, cherished by children and adults alike. Abol Tabol remains a cultural treasure, and Sandesh continues under his family’s legacy. His works are celebrated in Bengali literary festivals, and his influence is seen in modern Bengali children’s literature and theater.

Famous Quotes

“In nonsense lies the freedom of imagination.”
“A child’s laughter is the heart of poetry.”
“Words can dance when the mind is free.”

Timeline of Important Events

  • 1887: Born in Kolkata, West Bengal.
  • 1911–1914: Studied printing technology in England.
  • 1915: Took over editorship of Sandesh magazine.
  • 1921: Published HaJaBaRaLa.
  • 1922: Published Lakkhaner Shaktishel.
  • 1923: Published Abol Tabol.
  • 1923: Passed away in Kolkata.

Personal Life and Children

Sukumar Ray married Suprabha Das and had one son, Satyajit Ray, who became a legendary filmmaker. Living in Kolkata, Sukumar balanced his creative pursuits with running the family’s printing press and Sandesh magazine. His short life, cut short by illness at 35, was marked by boundless creativity and dedication to Bengali literature.
