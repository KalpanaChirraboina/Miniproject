Tibet Jathika Sikkim Mahinda Thero

Born: c. 1901, Gangtok, Sikkim

Died: March 16, 1951, Sri Lanka

State: Sikkim

Language: Sinhalese, Pali, English

Nationality: Indian

Education: Vidyodaya Pirivena, Maradana; Mahabodhi College, Colombo

Occupation: Buddhist Monk, Poet, Author

Notable Role: National Hero of Sri Lanka

Venerable S. Mahinda Thero, born in Sikkim, was a Buddhist monk, poet, and author who became a national hero in Sri Lanka. Known as Tibet Jathika Sikkim Mahinda Thero, he arrived in Ceylon (now Sri Lanka) as a young scholar and mastered Sinhalese to craft patriotic poetry that inspired the independence movement against British rule. His works, blending Buddhist values with calls for freedom, continue to resonate in Sri Lankan literature and culture.

Books and Publications

  • Jathika Thotilla: Poems urging Sri Lankans to fight for freedom.
  • Nidhahase Manthraya: A collection celebrating national pride.
  • Lanka Mata: Patriotic verses honoring Sri Lanka’s heritage.

Early Life and Education

Born around 1901 in Gangtok, Sikkim, as Pempa Tendupi Serky Cherin, S. Mahinda grew up in a prominent family. His elder half-brother, Kazi Dawa Samdup, cared for the family after their father’s death. With a scholarship, he arrived in Ceylon (1912 or 1914) to study Buddhism, accompanied by his brother, Sikkim Punnaji. He studied at Vidyodaya Pirivena, Maradana, Mahabodhi College, Colombo, and the Island Hermitage, mastering Sinhalese, Pali, and English.

Monastic and Literary Career

Ordained into the Amarapura Nikaya as Sikkim Mahinda, he later joined the Shyamopali Nikaya in 1930. Fluent in Sinhalese, he authored over 40 books, including poetry collections like Ova Muthu Dama (1921) and Lanka Mata, which fueled Sri Lanka’s independence movement. He taught at Nalanda College, Colombo (1934–1936), and wrote children’s poems, patriotic verses, and translations like Sadhammopijana.

Literary Contributions

S. Mahinda’s poetry, written in Sinhalese, is celebrated for its patriotic fervor and Buddhist themes. His works, such as Jathika Thotilla and Nidhahase Manthraya, inspired Sri Lankans to resist British rule. He pioneered children’s religious poetry and wrote prose like Rahula Kumaraya. His verses remain in Sri Lankan school textbooks, cementing his literary legacy.

Selected Poems

  • Rathriya: A children’s poem using vivid imagery of stars as jasmine flowers.
  • Freedom Poem: “Freedom is a diamond crown, Religion is a diamond lamp…” (translated).
  • Poems from Jathika Thotilla: Rousing calls for national pride.
  • Poems from Nidhahase Manthraya: Celebrating Sri Lanka’s independence spirit.

Major Achievements

  • Recognized as a national hero of Sri Lanka for his literary contributions.
  • Authored over 40 books, inspiring the independence movement.
  • Honored with a 5-cent stamp by Sri Lanka’s Postal Department (1970s).
  • Pioneered children’s religious poetry in Sinhalese literature.
  • Active member of Sri Lanka’s temperance and independence movements.

Legacy and Honors

S. Mahinda’s poetry continues to inspire Sri Lankans, with his works included in school curricula. He was commemorated with a Sri Lankan postage stamp in the 1970s. His role in reviving Buddhism and promoting national pride earned him the title “Tibet Jathika Sikkim Mahinda Thero.” His ashes are reportedly preserved at Mahabellana temple, Panadura.

Famous Quotes

“Freedom is a diamond crown, Religion is a diamond lamp, If (someone is) able to protect these, It’s you my child.”
“Freedom is like the ocean, And you, dear son, is the fountain of it…”

Life

S. Mahinda, born Pempa Tendupi Serky Cherin, lived as a Buddhist monk, dedicating his life to literature and Sri Lanka’s independence. His family included three brothers, one of whom, Sikkim Punnaji, also became a monk. His childhood friend, Tizan Namgyal, became Sikkim’s king. He had no recorded spouse or children, living a monastic life.
