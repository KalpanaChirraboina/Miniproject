Sant Tukaram Profile Details

Sant Tukaram (1608–1650), born in Dehu, Maharashtra, was a revered Marathi poet-saint and a prominent figure in the Bhakti movement. His devotional poetry, known as Abhangs, composed in Marathi, expressed profound love for Lord Vithoba and critiqued social inequalities. A key influencer of the Varkari sect, Tukaram’s works, compiled in the Tukaram Gatha, continue to inspire spiritual and literary traditions in Maharashtra.

Books and Publications

  • Tukaram Gatha: A collection of his Abhangs, compiled posthumously.

Early Life and Education

Born in 1608 in Dehu, Maharashtra, to a family of farmers and traders, Tukaram faced early hardships, including the loss of his parents and first wife. Largely self-taught, he immersed himself in spiritual texts and the works of earlier saints like Namdev and Eknath, which shaped his devotional poetry and philosophy.

Literary Career

Tukaram’s literary career centered on composing Abhangs, short devotional poems sung in praise of Lord Vithoba. His works, later compiled as the Tukaram Gatha, addressed themes of devotion, equality, and social reform. Despite facing opposition from orthodox Brahmins, his poetry gained widespread popularity among the masses.

Literary Contributions

Tukaram’s Abhangs enriched Marathi literature with their simplicity, emotional depth, and spiritual insight. His poetry challenged caste hierarchies and promoted universal devotion, influencing the Bhakti movement. His works, preserved orally and later in the Tukaram Gatha, remain integral to Maharashtra’s cultural and spiritual heritage.

Selected Poems

  • Sundara Te Dhyan: A poem praising the beauty of Lord Vithoba.
  • Je Ka Ranjale Ganjale: A call for compassion towards the downtrodden.
  • Tuka Mhane: A reflective poem on devotion and self-realization.

Major Achievements

  • Composed over 4,000 Abhangs, enriching Marathi literature.
  • Strengthened the Varkari sect and Bhakti movement in Maharashtra.
  • Inspired later poets and saints, including Mahatma Gandhi and Shivaji Maharaj.

Legacy and Honors

Sant Tukaram’s legacy endures through his Abhangs, sung during Varkari pilgrimages to Pandharpur. His teachings influenced social reformers and freedom fighters. His works are studied in Marathi literature, and his life is celebrated in films, plays, and festivals like the Dehu Yatra in Maharashtra.

Famous Quotes

“He who shelters the poor and the helpless is the true devotee of God.”
“Words are the only jewels I possess; words are the only wealth I distribute.”

Timeline of Important Events

  • 1608: Born in Dehu, Maharashtra.
  • 1620s: Began composing Abhangs after spiritual awakening.
  • 1630s: Faced opposition from orthodox Brahmins for his egalitarian teachings.
  • 1650: Passed away in Dehu, believed to have ascended to Vaikuntha.

Personal Life and Children

Tukaram married twice; his first wife, Rakhumai, died during a famine, and his second wife was Jijabai. He had children, including a son named Mahadev. Despite personal tragedies, including poverty, Tukaram’s devotion to Vithoba and his poetry provided solace, shaping his spiritual and literary legacy.
