Rose Kerketta Profile Details

Rose Kerketta is a distinguished Adivasi poet and activist from Jharkhand, celebrated for her powerful poetry that amplifies the voices of tribal communities. Writing primarily in Hindi and Sadri, her works reflect the struggles, identity, and resilience of Adivasi people, addressing issues like displacement, cultural erosion, and social injustice. As a leading voice in Adivasi literature, she combines poignant lyricism with activism, advocating for tribal rights and cultural preservation.

Books and Publications

  • Gehun Sirf Roti Nahi (2008): A poetry collection highlighting Adivasi life and struggles.
  • Bhukhi Dharti Ka Dukh (2012): Poems reflecting environmental and social issues faced by tribals.
  • Adivasi Saans Ki Tarah (2016): A collection celebrating Adivasi identity and resistance.
  • Jangal Ke Geet (2020): Poems inspired by the forests and tribal culture of Jharkhand.

Early Life and Education

Rose Kerketta was born in 1960 in Ranchi, Jharkhand, into an Adivasi community with deep ties to the land and its traditions. Growing up in a region marked by tribal culture and socio-economic challenges, she developed a strong sense of identity and purpose. She graduated from Ranchi University, where her exposure to literature and social movements shaped her poetic voice and commitment to Adivasi rights.

Literary Career

Rose Kerketta began her literary career by writing poetry that gave voice to the marginalized Adivasi communities of Jharkhand. Her works, written in Hindi and Sadri, explore themes of displacement, environmental destruction, and cultural survival. She has published in various literary journals and has become a prominent figure in Adivasi literature, using her poetry as a tool for activism and awareness.
