Jyoti Prasad Agarwala Profile Details

Jyoti Prasad Agarwala, revered as the "Rupkonwar" (Prince of Beauty) of Assamese culture, was a multifaceted artist whose contributions as a poet, playwright, filmmaker, and composer shaped modern Assamese identity. Born in Tamulbari, Assam, he pioneered Assamese cinema with his film Joymoti (1935) and enriched literature with patriotic and romantic poetry. His works instilled cultural pride and played a key role in India’s freedom movement.

Books and Publications

  • Jyoti Sangeet (1937): A collection of songs and poems.
  • Karengar Ligiri (1927): A romantic play.
  • Rupkonwar Kobita (1940): A collection of poems.

Early Life and Education

Jyoti Prasad Agarwala was born on June 17, 1903, in Tamulbari Tea Estate, Assam, to a prominent family. His father, Paramananda Agarwala, was a tea planter. Exposed to Assamese culture and arts early on, he studied at Edinburgh University, Scotland, where he explored global literature and cinema, shaping his vision for Assamese cultural revival.

Literary Career

Agarwala’s literary contributions include poetry, plays, and songs that blended romanticism with patriotism. His collection Jyoti Sangeet (1937) introduced a unique musical style, while plays like Karengar Ligiri (1927) explored love and social themes. His works inspired Assamese youth and strengthened cultural identity during the freedom struggle.

Social and Political Activism

A fervent supporter of India’s independence movement, Agarwala used his art to mobilize Assamese people against British rule. He was imprisoned for his involvement in the Quit India Movement. His films and writings promoted cultural pride and social reform, advocating for Assamese identity and unity.

Selected Poems

  • Luitor Paaror Geet (1937): A patriotic song celebrating the Brahmaputra.
  • Mor Desh (1940): A poem of love for Assam.
  • Rupkonwaror Xur (1937): A romantic and lyrical poem.
  • Assamiya Dekar Xapon (1940): A poem envisioning Assam’s future.
  • Joymotir Geet (1935): A song inspired by his film Joymoti.

Major Achievements

  • Pioneered Assamese cinema with Joymoti (1935), the first Assamese film.
  • Created Jyoti Sangeet, a unique genre of Assamese music.
  • Contributed to India’s freedom movement through art and activism.
  • Authored influential plays and poems shaping modern Assamese literature.

Legacy and Honors

Jyoti Prasad Agarwala’s legacy endures as a cornerstone of Assamese culture. His contributions are celebrated through the Jyoti Prasad Agarwala Award and annual cultural festivals. His films, songs, and writings continue to inspire Assamese artists and foster cultural pride.

Famous Quotes

Personal Life and Children

Jyoti Prasad Agarwala married Devajani Bhuyan in 1930 and had five children: three daughters, Radhika, Kamala, and Jaya, and two sons, Pratap and Satyajit. His life was dedicated to art, activism, and the cultural upliftment of Assam.
