Subramania Bharati

Subramania Bharati

The Mahakavi of Tamil Nadu

Born: December 11, 1882, Ettayapuram

Died: September 11, 1921, Chennai

State: Tamil Nadu

Language: Tamil

Nationality: Indian

Education: Hindu College, Tirunelveli

Occupation: Poet, Journalist, Freedom Fighter

Notable Role: Pioneer of Modern Tamil Poetry

Subramania Bharati, fondly known as Mahakavi Bharati, was a revolutionary Tamil poet, journalist, and freedom fighter whose visionary works ignited the spirit of Indian independence. Born in Ettayapuram, Tamil Nadu, his poetry blended patriotism, social reform, and spiritualism, reshaping modern Tamil literature. As a fearless advocate for women’s rights and equality, Bharati’s legacy continues to inspire millions with its passion and universality.

Books and Publications

  • Bharathiyar Kavithaigal (Various Years): A collection of his patriotic and lyrical poems.
  • Panchali Sapatham (1912): An epic poem inspired by the Mahabharata, symbolizing India’s freedom struggle.
  • Kannan Pattu (1917): Devotional poems dedicated to Lord Krishna.
  • Kuyil Pattu (1917): A poetic narrative celebrating nature and freedom (available on Archive.org).

Early Life and Education

Subramania Bharati was born on December 11, 1882, in Ettayapuram, Tamil Nadu, to a Brahmin family. His father, Chinnaswami Subramania Iyer, was a court official, and his mother, Lakshmi Ammal, passed away when he was five. A child prodigy, Bharati earned the title “Bharati” (blessed by Saraswati) at age 11 for his poetic talent. He studied at Hindu College, Tirunelveli, and was well-versed in Tamil, Sanskrit, and English, drawing inspiration from both Indian and Western literary traditions.

Political Career

Bharati joined the Indian independence movement in the early 1900s, advocating for Swaraj (self-rule). He edited nationalist journals like Swadesamitran and India, spreading revolutionary ideas. His fiery writings led to British surveillance, forcing him to flee to Pondicherry (1908–1918), a French territory, where he continued his activism. Influenced by leaders like Bal Gangadhar Tilak, Bharati’s poetry inspired mass resistance against colonial rule.

Literary Contributions

Bharati revolutionized Tamil literature with his accessible, modern style, blending classical Tamil with contemporary themes. His poetry covered patriotism (Vande Mataram translations), social reform, women’s emancipation, and spirituality. Works like Panchali Sapatham and Kannan Pattu showcase his versatility. As a journalist, he wrote essays on nationalism and equality, shaping public discourse.

Selected Poems

  • Achamillai Achamillai (1910): A fearless anthem of courage and defiance.
  • Bharat Mata Ashtakam (1910): A tribute to Mother India’s glory.
  • Viduthalai Viduthalai (1910): A passionate call for freedom.
  • Ninnai Charan Adainthen (1917): A devotional poem to Goddess Shakti.
  • Pudiya Aathichudi (1914): A modern ethical guide for children.

Major Achievements

  • Pioneered modern Tamil poetry and prose.
  • Inspired India’s freedom struggle through patriotic writings.
  • Advocated women’s rights and social equality in early 20th-century India.
  • Translated Sanskrit texts and English works into Tamil, enriching the language.
  • Posthumously honored with statues and memorials across Tamil Nadu.

Legacy and Honors

Bharati’s legacy endures through his poetry, which remains a rallying cry for freedom and equality. His birth anniversary, December 11, is celebrated as Bharati Day in Tamil Nadu. The Subramania Bharati Award recognizes literary excellence. Statues in Chennai and Ettayapuram, along with a 1987 Indian postage stamp, commemorate his contributions.

Famous Quotes

“There is no fear, no fear at all!”
“India will rise to the pinnacle of freedom and glory.”
“Knowledge is the sword that destroys ignorance.”

Timeline of Important Events

  • 1882: Born in Ettayapuram, Tamil Nadu.
  • 1897: Conferred the title “Bharati” for poetic prowess.
  • 1904: Began journalistic career with Swadesamitran.
  • 1908: Fled to Pondicherry to escape British arrest.
  • 1912: Published Panchali Sapatham.
  • 1917: Released Kannan Pattu and Kuyil Pattu.
  • 1918: Returned to British India, arrested briefly.
  • 1921: Passed away in Chennai.

Personal Life and Children

Bharati married Chellamma in 1897, at age 14, in an arranged marriage. They had two daughters, Thangammal and Shakuntala. Despite financial struggles and exile, Bharati remained devoted to his family. His progressive views on women’s education influenced his household, with Chellamma supporting his literary and revolutionary pursuits.
